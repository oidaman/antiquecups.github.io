The collection and the families

Antique cups are frequently collected and admired for their beautiful craftsmanship, colours and shapes but often the most interesting and historical aspects are entirely overlooked. These cups were often once owned by renowned and powerful families, with interesting stories and histories. I have built a cup collection that is not only interesting to look at but also fascinating to dwell into the history of these porcelain marvels. I have spent many decades compiling and researching these families, this website is a way to keep their legacies alive and show the world their once prized heirlooms.

The Wermacht Family Cup

A tale of blood and violence

A rather innocent looking cup, however the Wermacht family went through a great deal of pain due to this porcelain marvel. Acquired by Otto Wermacht in 1879 in Austria from a deceased friend, Otto’s wife took a quick liking to the cup and had it displayed by her bedside table. One night in 1880 her throat was slit in the night, the Austrian high council tried Otto for the murder of his wife, and he was hanged twelve days later. His son Wolfgang inherited the cup and passed it onto his wife as a proclamation of his love to her.

The Humberton Family Cup

A story of unbelievable luck and fortune

A family of craftsmen and lumberjacks, living in a sort of commune in the forest, a simplistic sustainable lifestyle. Hence the ownership of such a rich object brings surprise. The cup is an object of mystery supposedly it was found tangled in the trunk of a felled oak, and family legend says that after this relic was unearthed the commune began to flourish, crops began growing in great yield and abundance, and children were being cured of sickness. The cup quickly became an object of worship.

The Francois Family Cup

An artistic perspective

An artistic syndicate the Francois were a well-known family in Paris for their eccentric behaviours and influence in fashion and art during the 18th and 19th century. The cup was the creation of the illegitimate-child of Anton Francois as a way to show the family that he was still very much gifted despite the lack of surname.

The McKaw Family Cup

A drunk tale

From a small village south of Dublin during the Great Famine, a Scottish-Irish family of drunks acquired the cup after Oisin McKaw bet the owner of local tavern that he could finish two pints of bourbon in under a minute. For winning he would get the taverns most exquisite object, a cup gifted by a Japanese traveller, if he lost he would forfeit his ‘Sir’ title to the tavern owner. Oisin won the bet, however he paid for it with his life – two days later he was found dead in his bed, it was said the hangover had killed him.

The Kosolovsky Family Cup

A story of escape

Caught in the chaos of the USSR a once royal and wealthy family had their lands and riches stripped by Stalin government due to their very open proclamation of faith. This cup was one of the few items smuggled into borders of Poland, due to having great significance to the family as it was part of Alexander II the Liberators wife’s royal collection. Interestingly this cup was found and purchased in a garage sale in a small village in Poland.

The Cook Family Cup

The life of crime

The Cooks a known crime family from Manchester, England. Robbing people, controlling gambling rings, rigging horse races and boxing matches, the Cooks had their hands in any remotely nefarious business. This cup was their family heirloom and was sold in auction after all their assets were seized and all members brought to hanging.

The Tarnet Family Cup

A story of goodwill

A green thumb family; growing flowers, fruit and vegetables for a small town in New Caledonia. The town mayor commissioned the creation of this cup using the finest china and real gold leaf as a thank you for the centuries of service the Tarnets had provided.

The Gelder Family Cup

Nomads and migrants

Little is known about the Gelders, only what was found in a diary that was found in a chest containing this very cup buried in the Oltenian Sahara Desert. A family of nomads trading resources between oasis towns by camel, where the cup is from and what significance it held remains a mystery.
